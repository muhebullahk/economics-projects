Teaching Philosophy

Approach to economics education

My teaching philosophy centres on connecting economic theory to real-world phenomena through data and rigorous analysis. I believe that economics is most effectively learned when students engage actively with models, question assumptions, and apply tools to genuine policy questions. I design courses to build cumulative analytical skills, from intuition-building at the introductory level to formal modelling and empirical work at upper-division and graduate levels.

I emphasise written and quantitative communication alongside economic reasoning, and I strive to create an inclusive, challenging classroom environment where every student can develop confidence in economic analysis.
