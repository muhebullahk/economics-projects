ECON 230 — Introduction to Behavioural Economics

Topic Pages

Interactive explainers and tools for key topics in behavioural economics.

Foundations

🧠
Foundations: Limits of Rationality

The rational agent model, expected utility theory, why standard assumptions fail in practice, and bounded rationality — the idea that people are intentionally rational but cognitively constrained.

Heuristics and Biases

Kahneman & Tversky's research program — System 1 vs System 2, the representativeness heuristic, availability heuristic, anchoring effects, confirmation bias, and overconfidence.

Open interactive →

Risk & Time

📉
Prospect Theory and Decision Under Risk

Kahneman & Tversky's replacement for expected utility — reference dependence, loss aversion, diminishing sensitivity, and probability weighting. The S-shaped value function and the probability weighting function.

Open interactive →
⏳
Time Inconsistency and Present Bias

Hyperbolic vs. exponential discounting, the β-δ model, why people procrastinate and undersave, and how commitment devices help the present self constrain the future self.

Open interactive →
💰
Intertemporal Choice and Savings Behaviour

Consumption-savings decisions, retirement planning, and why people systematically undersave. Mental accounting — how people separate money into distinct "mental buckets" — and its effects on spending and saving.

Open interactive →

Social & Strategic Behaviour

🤝
Social Preferences

Departures from pure self-interest — fairness, inequality aversion, positive and negative reciprocity, altruism, and cooperation. The ultimatum game, dictator game, and Fehr-Schmidt model.

Open interactive →
♟️
Behavioural Game Theory

Trust and cooperation in strategic settings. Ultimatum, public goods, and trust games — and why outcomes deviate from Nash equilibrium predictions. Learning rules and bounded rationality in repeated games.

Open interactive →

Markets & Finance

📈
Market Implications and Behavioural Finance

Asset bubbles, herding, and investor sentiment. Overreaction and underreaction in financial markets. Noise traders vs rational traders — and why arbitrage may not correct mispricings.

Open interactive →

Policy & Applications

🎯
Nudges and Choice Architecture

Associated with Richard Thaler and the book Nudge — how the design of choice environments shapes decisions. Default effects, framing effects, simplification and salience, and the distinction between policy nudges and mandates.

Open interactive →
🏛️
Applications and Policy Design

Behavioural insights applied to health (vaccination, smoking, obesity), education choices, and tax compliance. Designing better institutions using behavioural economics — from public policy to organisational design.
