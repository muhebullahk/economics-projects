Assistant Professor of Economics

Dr. Muhebullah Karimzada

Faculty of Business and Economics — University of Northern British Columbia

Welcome. I am an economist specializing in macroeconomics, with a particular focus on business cycles and the housing market. My research explores the dynamics of economic fluctuations, the interplay between housing markets and broader economic conditions, and the policy implications of these relationships. I teach a broad range of economics courses at UNBC.

Research Interests

Core areas of active inquiry

Macroeconomics

Business cycle dynamics, aggregate fluctuations, and the macroeconomic determinants of growth and stability.

Housing Markets

Housing investment, the interplay between housing markets and broader economic conditions, and policy implications.

Monetary Economics

International monetary economics, monetary policy transmission, and open-economy macroeconomics.

Labour Economics

Labour market dynamics in the construction sector and the relationship between housing cycles and employment.

DSGE Modelling

Bayesian estimation of dynamic stochastic general equilibrium models applied to business cycle analysis.

Euro Area Economics

Business cycle synchronisation, structural shocks, and macroeconomic policy in the Euro Area.
