Assistant Professor of Economics

Dr. Muhebullah Karimzada

Department of Economics — University of Northern British Columbia

Welcome. I am an economist specializing in applied microeconomics, labour economics, and public policy. My research examines how economic institutions, incentives, and policies shape individual outcomes and social welfare. I also teach a broad range of undergraduate and graduate economics courses at UNBC.

Research Interests

Core areas of active inquiry

Applied Microeconomics

Empirical analysis of individual and firm decision-making, market design, and efficiency.

Labour Economics

Wage determination, labour market policy, employment dynamics, and human capital formation.

Public Economics

Taxation, government expenditure, social insurance, and the efficiency of public programs.

Development Economics

Poverty, inequality, institutional quality, and economic growth in developing economies.

Econometrics

Causal inference, program evaluation, panel data methods, and applied statistical modelling.

Industrial Organization

Market structure, firm conduct, competition policy, and regulation.
